<p><p>The Water Corrosion Inhibitor Market includes two primary types: Inorganic and Organic. Inorganic inhibitors, such as phosphates, silicates, and chromates, form protective layers on metal surfaces, preventing corrosion through chemical reactions. They are effective in high-temperature and high-pressure environments. Organic inhibitors, like amines and carboxylates, adsorb onto metal surfaces, creating a barrier against corrosive elements. They are eco-friendly and versatile, suitable for various water systems. Both types aim to extend equipment lifespan and reduce maintenance costs, catering to industries like oil & gas, power generation, and water treatment.</p></p>
