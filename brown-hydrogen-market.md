<p><p>Brown hydrogen is produced through the gasification of coal or lignite, a process that emits carbon dioxide (CO2) as a byproduct. Unlike green hydrogen, which is produced using renewable energy, brown hydrogen is considered less environmentally friendly due to its reliance on fossil fuels and significant carbon emissions. Despite this, it remains a cost-effective method for hydrogen production, particularly in regions with abundant coal reserves.</p><p>The Brown Hydrogen Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 11.5% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing demand for hydrogen in industries such as refining, chemicals, and transportation, where it is used as a feedstock or fuel. The affordability of coal-based hydrogen production, especially in developing economies, further supports market expansion. However, environmental concerns and the global push toward decarbonization are prompting a gradual shift toward cleaner alternatives like green and blue hydrogen.</p><p>Recent trends in the brown hydrogen market include advancements in carbon capture, utilization, and storage (CCUS) technologies, which aim to reduce the carbon footprint of coal-based hydrogen production. Additionally, government policies and investments in hydrogen infrastructure are influencing market dynamics, with some regions prioritizing brown hydrogen as a transitional solution while scaling up renewable energy capacity. Despite its challenges, brown hydrogen continues to play a role in the evolving hydrogen economy.</p></p>
