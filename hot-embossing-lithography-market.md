<p><p>Hot Embossing Lithography is a precise manufacturing technique used to create microstructures on substrates by applying heat and pressure to an embossing stamp. The integration of this technology in flexible electronics, microfluidics, and lab-on-a-chip devices is particularly noteworthy, reflecting a shift towards miniaturization and enhanced functionality.</p><p>Additionally, the rising focus on sustainable manufacturing processes is beneficial for the market, as hot embossing can consume less energy compared to traditional lithography methods.
