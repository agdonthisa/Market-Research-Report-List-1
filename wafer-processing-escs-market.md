In contrast, the Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type employs a combination of mechanical and electrostatic methods, facilitating more effective cleaning processes. Both types cater to the increasing demand for higher yield and lower defect rates in wafer fabrication, playing crucial roles in advancing semiconductor technology and boosting overall production efficiency.</p></p>
