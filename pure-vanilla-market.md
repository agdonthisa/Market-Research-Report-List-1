<p><p>The pure vanilla market comprises four main types: bean, extract, powder, and paste. Vanilla beans are the whole pods and offer the most intense flavor, typically used in high-end culinary applications. Extract is made by steeping beans in alcohol, providing concentrated flavor for baking and beverages. Vanilla powder is created by grinding dried beans, ideal for dry mixes. Vanilla paste combines the essence of extract with ground beans, offering a convenient option for recipes requiring both flavor and texture.</p></p>
